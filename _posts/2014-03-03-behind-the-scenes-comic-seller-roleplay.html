It's been a while since my last Behind The Scenes article but I don't want to write one if there are no particular differences with the others recording sessions.

But recently I changed a little my way of working so here I am to talk to you about it!

As you may know, I bought a binaural microphone : the 3Dio free space pro. Obviously, that changes a lot of things and my first videos with this new device were quite funny to do!

Here is a picture of my first try, during the recording of the counterfeiter roleplay.

[caption id="attachment_325" align="aligncenter" width="800"] "I'm falliiiiinnnnng"[/caption]

The microphone didn't want to remain standing because of the chair, the pillow and the folder. I thought I had to let it fell and record like this, so I did that.

But today I absolutely wanted to find another solution, bought a new brain, and found this :

[caption id="attachment_327" align="aligncenter" width="768"] This giant box was 10 feet away from my recording location ...[/caption]

A big box, a small box (with a decoder inside) and a comic book to have a really "hard" surface and here it is! It's higher than before, way more stable and it's the perfect size for the microphone/phone dynamic duo.

[caption id="attachment_329" align="aligncenter" width="768"] It looks awesome when it's not falling[/caption]

It was a pleasure to record in these conditions, it's still the best place in my house to record and I can easily do some tests with my wireless headset!

[caption id="attachment_330" align="aligncenter" width="538"] Fuzzy but ready to conquer the world! [/caption]
